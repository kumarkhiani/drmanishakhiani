Making Homeopathy Medicines

Homeopathic Medicines are prepared from Plants, Animals and Minerals and are extremely diluted.

Homeopathic medicines are made by diluting a medicinal substance in a double distilled water. Each substance is diluted, most commonly 1 part of the original medicinal agent to 9 or 99 parts double distilled water.

The mixture is then vigorously shaken. The solution is then again diluted 1:9 or 1:99 and vigorously shaken.

This process of consecutive diluting and shaking is repeated 3, 6, 12, 30, 200, 1000 or more…

Thus Homeopathic medicines are “potentized” (the process of sequential dilution with vigorous shaking in between each dilution). In homeopathy, a solution that is more dilute is described as having a higher potency. More diluted substance are considered by homeopaths to be stronger and deep acting.

30C and below are called as low potencies and 200 is called as moderate potency. 1M and above are called as Higher potencies.
